STICKSProduct + Technical SpecificationVersion 2.0  č  Confidential1. Product OverviewSticks is built on two co-equal pillars. The first is a white-label tournament and league engine that lets any golf organization č a tour company, a high school athletics program, a golf academy, a private club č run their entire competitive season through Sticks. The second is a social feed that functions like a live sports ticker crossed with a group chat, driving daily engagement for individual players whether or not they are in a tournament that day.No existing golf app has solved daily retention outside of active rounds. Every competitor is a utility players open on the course and close when they walk off. Sticks is built to be the app golfers check every morning the same way they check a sports scores app č because something always happened overnight. Leaderboard moves, bet settlements, crew activity, tournament results, rivalry updates. The feed is the engine that makes this possible.Organizations bring players onto the platform through tournaments and leagues. The social feed and crew system are what keep those players opening the app every day after the event is over. These two pillars reinforce each other.AttributeDetailPrimary product č OrganizationsWhite-label tournament + league management enginePrimary product č PlayersSocial feed driving daily engagement outside of active roundsTarget organizationsAmateur tours and circuits, high school programs, golf academies, private clubs, corporate leaguesPlayer experienceLive social feed, leaderboards, GPS scoring, crew system, rivalries, side betsLaunch marketUtah č high school programs + local leaguesRevenue modelOrg licensing + tournament entry rake + player subscriptions + tee time commission2. Core Player FeaturesThese features serve the individual golfer on the platform. They exist to drive player retention, daily engagement, and organic crew growth. They are the reason players open Sticks on days they are not in a tournament.3.1 Onboarding + Authź Sign up / sign in: Apple, Google, phone, emailź Profile setup: name, avatar, play style (Competitive / Casual / Social)ź Handicap: link GHIN (preferred) or enter manually. GHIN keeps index auto-synced.ź Home course selectionź Player discovery: contacts sync, Instagram connect, X/Twitter connectź Membership pitch at end of onboarding3.2 GPS + Live Scoringź Session-based round tracking: starts and stops with the roundź Course maps: Google Maps SDK as the base layer, overlaid with iGolf course data (vector shapes, hole layouts, green outlines, elevation) č iGolf API acquisition TBD, GolfCourseAPI.com used for dev and beta phasesź Distance to pin: front, center, back č powered by iGolf course geometryź Hole-by-hole scorecard: strokes, putts, penaltiesź GPS timestamps recorded per hole as proof-of-play (supports bet integrity + official scoring)ź Background location on iOS: session continues with screen lockedź Offline-first: round state persists locally and syncs when signal returnsNote: The map layer is Google Maps (base) + iGolf (course-specific data: hole shapes, pin positions, elevation, green detail). iGolf API contract is pending č GolfCourseAPI.com covers dev and beta. Architecture must be built so the course data source is swappable without touching the map rendering logic.3.3 Social FeedThe social feed is a co-primary product alongside the tournament engine. It is the reason players open Sticks on days they are not competing. The design goal is a live sports ticker crossed with a group chat č there is always something new because every platform action generates a feed event. A player who follows 20 other golfers has a feed that is constantly moving.ź Every platform action emits a FeedEvent: leaderboard move, bet settlement, tournament result, round completion, rivalry milestone, crew activity, bracket advancementź Feed renders as a live ticker č newest events at top, real-time push via Supabase Realtime (no pull-to-refresh required)ź Crew-scoped feed: see only activity from your crewź Global feed: broader platform activity, surfaces interesting moments across the appź Tournament feed: scoped to a specific event, useful for spectators and participantsź Rivalry cards: head-to-head record between two players surfaces automatically in the feed when they play each otherź Push notifications mirror the feed č a leaderboard move by a rival at 9pm on a Tuesday is the kind of moment that pulls players back into the appNote: The FeedEvent architecture is one of the most important technical decisions in the project. Every feature must emit to the feed. This should be designed as a first-class event bus from the start, not bolted on later. A poorly architected feed is hard to retrofit.3.4 Crews + Rivalriesź Create and join crews (friend groups or teams)ź Crew leaderboard: season stats and ranking among membersź Rivalry tracking: head-to-head record between two playersź Player profiles: round history, stats, rivalry recordsź Deep linking: crew invites and leaderboard shares open the correct in-app screen3.5 Side Bets + Game Formats (Secondary Feature)Side bets are a supporting engagement feature, not the core product. They exist to make casual rounds more competitive and give the social feed more content to surface. They are explicitly not the primary revenue mechanism.ź 14+ formats: Skins, Nassau, Wolf, Bingo Bango Bongo, Stableford, Match Play, Vegas, and moreź Prop bets: closest to pin, longest drive, fewest puttsź Stripe escrow: funds held at bet creation, auto-settled at round end after dispute windowź Bet history and P&L per playerNote: LEGAL RISK: Real-money bet settlement will be scrutinized in Apple App Store review. Must be framed as skill-based game formats with optional stakes. Legal review required before build. This feature should be scoped after the tournament engine is stable.3.6 Tee Time Bookingź Search by date, course, time, number of playersź foreUP as primary integration (Utah-dominant tee sheet software)ź GolfNow / Chronogolf as national fallbackź Sticks takes commission on every booking č primary free-tier revenueź Push reminders: 24hr and 2hr before tee time3.7 Membership TiersTierPricePrimary AccessFreečTee time booking.
